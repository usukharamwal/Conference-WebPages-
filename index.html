Welcome to FAIM 2025 International Conference

The FAIM (Flexible Automation and Intelligent Manufacturing) international conference is well-known for its role in advancing research, theories, and practices in automation and manufacturing. Over the years, FAIM has proven to offer valuable insights into technological advancements and managerial strategies. By providing scientific sessions, workshops, tutorials, and industry tours, the FAIM platform offers a comprehensive connection for academics and industry professionals. Throughout its history, FAIM has been hosted by several universities across Europe, America, and Asia. The FAIM conference selects approximately 250-300 high-quality research papers through an accurate and strict peer-review process, published by hundreds of influential researchers in automation and manufacturing. This encourages cooperation between researchers to bridge the gap between research and practical application.

Binghamton University

FAIM 2025 is hosted by Binghamton University (BU), one of the four public research universities in the State University of New York (SUNY). BU is well known for its academic excellence and innovative research. BU is located in the southern region of New York State. The location of BU allows for a convenient and short gateway to major cities like New York, Boston, and Philadelphia. The thriving community and rich cultural scene make BU an attractive destination for students, offering numerous opportunities for professional and personal growth. The beauty of the city's nature makes it an ideal place for academic achievement and outdoor activities.

Theme and Topics

The theme of FAIM 2025 is “The Future of Automation & Manufacturing: Intelligence, Agility, and Sustainability.”

Topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Flexible Automation Technologies and Collaborative Automation
  • AI for Intelligent Manufacturing and Dynamic Decision-Making
  • Digital Twins and Digital Twin-Driven Smart Manufacturing
  • Cybersecurity in Manufacturing and Data Protection
  • Sustainable Supply Chains and The Hyper-Connected Supply Chain
  • The Human Factor in Intelligent Manufacturing
  • The Impact of Emerging Technologies in Manufacturing
  • Production Planning and Scheduling and On-Demand Manufacturing
  • Resilient Manufacturing
  • Global Manufacturing Collaboration
  • The Ethical Framework for Smart Manufacturing

High Visibility Refereed Publications

All accepted papers presented at FAIM 2025 will be peer-reviewed and published in either Lecture Notes in Mechanical Engineering (Springer) or Manufacturing Letters (Elsevier), both indexed by Scopus. Authors submitting full-length manuscripts for potential Fast-Track publication must clearly indicate the journal they wish to be considered for:

  • Robotics and Computer-Integrated Manufacturing (Elsevier)
  • International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (Springer)
  • Journal of Manufacturing Systems (SME, Elsevier)

There is a strict policy limiting a single author to a maximum of five affiliated manuscripts across the conference submissions and only one full-length manuscript for fast-track journal publication. Please note that all accepted papers must be presented at the conference. Failure to present will result in the removal of the paper from the conference proceedings, IJAMT, RCIM, or any associated publication outlet.

FAIM2025 Special Awards Announcement

We proudly introduce the Professor Bill Sullivan Special Session on Green & Sustainable Manufacturing and Engineering Systems. This session honors the legacy of Professor Sullivan, a visionary in sustainable manufacturing.

Sullivan Best Paper Award:

Authors presenting in this special session will have the distinguished opportunity to compete for the Sullivan Best Paper Award, recognizing exceptional research contributions in green and sustainable manufacturing.

This prestigious award is sponsored by the Sullivan family, honoring innovation and excellence in manufacturing research, particularly in the pivotal areas of sustainability and green engineering.

We invite many participants to submit their finest work to commemorate Professor Sullivan’s enduring impact and contribute to advancing sustainable manufacturing practices.

Srihari Best Paper Award:

It recognizes innovative advancements that enhance efficiency and technological implementation in the industry. This prestigious honor celebrates outstanding research in the industrial applications of automation and manufacturing.

The award will be presented by Dr. Krishnaswami Srihari, SUNY Distinguished Professor and founder of WISE, in recognition of significant contributions to applied manufacturing research .

Best FAIM 2025 Conference Paper Award:

Presented to the most exceptional paper across all sessions, this award acknowledges groundbreaking contributions to the field of flexible automation and intelligent manufacturing.
