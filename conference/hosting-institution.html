Hosting Institution

Binghamton University (Visit Our Website)

Binghamton University, a top public research university in the State University of New York (SUNY) system, will host FAIM 2025. Ranked in the Top 10 among the “New Ivies” by Forbes in 2024 for producing talented, driven graduates sought after by employers across industries, Binghamton also holds the distinction of being the #1 public university in New York, according to Forbes 2024. With over 20 consecutive years as a top 50 public in the nation(U.S. News & World Report), Binghamton is recognized as the #1 best value college in New York (U.S. News & World Report, 2025). Located in southern New York State, the university is easily accessible from major cities like New York, Boston, and Philadelphia. Known for its strong academics, vibrant community, and beautiful natural surroundings, Binghamton provides an ideal setting for FAIM 2025 attendees, blending rigorous learning experiences with opportunities for outdoor exploration.

Vision

Binghamton as an institution is dedicated to higher education, one that combines an international reputation for graduate education, research, scholarship and creative endeavor with the best undergraduate programs available at any public university.

Mission

Binghamton University is committed to collaborative transdisciplinary research, inspirational artistic endeavors and high-impact educational experiences within an environment that advances diversity, equity and inclusion; international perspectives; and community engagement.

School of Systems Science and Industrial Engineering (SSIE) (Visit Our Website)

The School of Systems Science and Industrial Engineering has secured $8.5-12 million in research funding in the 2020-2024 fiscal years, highlighting the school's growth and success. This sustained funding underscores its dedication to cutting-edge research and innovation.

Watson Institute for Systems Excellence (WISE)

The Watson Institute for Systems Excellence (WISE), which is part of the Systems Science and Industrial Engineering (SSIE) School, is renowned for its applied research in collaboration with industry. WISE brings together master's, PhD, and some undergraduate students with federal and industry sponsors to conduct innovative, industry-focused research. Students gain real-life experience, build invaluable contacts, and work alongside distinguished faculty on cutting-edge projects. WISE develops customized solutions for industry challenges, utilizing a range of traditional and advanced methodologies.

Funding

  • Over $50 million cumulative funds since 1989
  • Exceeded $1 million per year since 1999
  • $3-4 million annual funds from sponsors for the last five years
  • 25 long-term partners nationally & internationally

Research Numbers

  • 700+ contracts completed
  • 500+ journal articles published
  • 250 annual research projects conducted
  • 2,000+ presentations made
  • 250+ dissertations / 750+ theses advised

Meet Our Founder!

Distinguished Professor Krishnaswami Srihari has served as dean of the Thomas J. Watson School of Engineering and Applied Science at Binghamton University since 2009 and was appointed executive vice provost for international initiatives and chief global affairs officer in 2015. A leading expert in electronics packaging and manufacturing, Srihari has also pioneered research applying systems engineering principles to improve healthcare delivery efficiency. Since joining Binghamton in 1988, he has been instrumental in building university-industry partnerships as the director of the Watson Institute of Systems Excellence (WISE). Srihari’s academic contributions include publishing over 500 peer-reviewed papers, authoring over 1,000 technical reports, and guiding over 160 MS and 40 PhD students. He has secured more than $60 million in external funding and has been recognized as a SUNY Distinguished Professor, the highest academic rank in the SUNY system. Among his many accolades are the IIE Fellowship, the Chancellor's Award for Excellence in Scholarship, and the University Award for Excellence in International Education.
